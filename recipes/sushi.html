Sushi

One variant of sushi is the beginner-friendly California Roll. This can easily be made at home. If you care about serving your family with a delicious home-cooked meal, this recipe serves four people, unless you have teenage boys. In which case, double the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2.5 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 4 nori (seaweed sheets)
  • 8 imitation crab sticks
  • 8 imitation crab sticks
  • 2 avocado
  • 1 cucumber
  • 2 tablespoons wasabi
  • 0.5 cups soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds (optional)

Steps:

  1. Cook the rice: Rinse 2 cups sushi rice under cold water. Add to rice cooker with 2.5 cups water. Cook according to rice cooker instructions (usually 20 minutes)
  2. Season the rice: Mix 3 tablespoons rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons sugar, and 1 teaspoons salt together. Pour over cooked rice while still warm. Fold gently to combine. Let cool for 10 minutes
  3. Prepare ingredients: Slice 2 avocado into thin strips. Cut 8 imitation crab sticks and 1 cucumber into long thin sticks. Have 4 nori (seaweed sheets) sheets ready.
  4. Set up rolling mat: Place bamboo mat on clean surface. Put one 4 nori (seaweed sheets) sheet shiny-side down on mat.
  5. Add rice to nori: Spread thin layer of seasoned rice (about ¼ cup) on 4 nori (seaweed sheets), leaving ½-inch border at top.
  6. Add fillings: Place 2 pieces of 8 imitation crab sticks, 2-3 slices of 2 avocado, and 1-2 sticks of 1 cucumber in a line across the center of the rice.
  7. Roll the sushi: sing the mat, fold and roll tightly away from you. The exposed nori border will seal the roll. Apply water to seal if needed.
  8. Slice the roll: Using a sharp wet knife, cut roll into 6-8 pieces. Wipe knife between cuts for clean edges.
  9. Serve: Arrange on plate with 2 tablespoons wasabi and 0.5 cups soy sauce for dipping. Sprinkle 2 tablespoons sesame seeds (optional) if desired. Serve immediately while fresh!

Notes:

Use a bamboo sushi mat (very affordable). Keep knife wet while slicing. Don't overfill rolls. Rice temperature is important - let it cool before rolling or it will get soggy.
